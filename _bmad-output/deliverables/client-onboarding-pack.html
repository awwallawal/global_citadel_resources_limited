  7. Stage 1 Completion and Review We will hold a final review session to walk through all Stage 1 deliverables, confirm alignment, and discuss recommendations for Stage 2 and beyond.

Section Four
How We Work Together

Clear communication and well-defined processes make for a smoother project and better outcomes. Here is how our collaboration will work in practice.

  • 1
    Primary Communication: Email Our primary communication channel is email. All project-related discussions, decisions, and file sharing should go through email so that everything is documented and easy to reference later. I aim to respond to all emails within 24 business hours. If a matter is genuinely urgent, you are welcome to call or send a text message to +234 805 302 3862.
  • 2
    Shared Project Folder You will receive access to a shared project folder organised into clear sections: Deliverables (where finished work lives), Working Files (drafts and works in progress), Client Materials (files you provide to me), and Reference (research, inspiration, and strategy documents). Please upload all materials you want me to work with into the Client Materials folder rather than sending them as email attachments, especially if the files are large.
  • 3
    How Deliverables Will Be Presented Design concepts and visual deliverables will be shared as high-quality PDF previews for your review. Once designs are approved, interactive prototypes and live preview links will be provided where appropriate so you can experience the design in a browser. All deliverable presentations will include a brief written explanation of the thinking behind each design decision.
  • 4
    How to Give Feedback Good feedback keeps the project moving efficiently. Please consolidate feedback from all stakeholders into a single, unified response rather than sending multiple rounds of conflicting comments. Be as specific as possible. For example, "The heading font feels too formal for our brand" is much more useful than "I do not like the fonts." All feedback should come through the designated primary point of contact to avoid confusion.
  • 5
    Weekly Status Updates Every week, you will receive a brief status update by email covering: what was completed since the last update, what is currently in progress, what is coming up next, and whether I need anything from you. This ensures you are never left wondering about the state of your project.
Response times matter both ways. I commit to responding within 24 business hours. To keep the project on schedule, I ask that you also aim to provide feedback and requested materials within the timeframes we agree upon. Delays in feedback or content can push the overall timeline, so prompt responses help us both.
