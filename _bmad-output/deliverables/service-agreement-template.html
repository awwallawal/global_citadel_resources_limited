Section 2

Background and Context

The Client owns and operates a conglomerate based in Nigeria with seven distinct business verticals, managed from the United Kingdom. The Client requires comprehensive web design, brand identity, and digital strategy services to establish or strengthen the digital presence of these businesses.

The Service Provider is a freelance web designer and developer based in Nigeria with experience serving UK and international clients. The Service Provider has been engaged to deliver these services across a phased, multi-stage project structure as described in this Agreement.

This Agreement covers Stage 1 (Foundation) in full and sets out the framework for subsequent stages. Each further stage will be governed by its own supplementary agreement, building on the foundation laid in Stage 1.

Section 3

Scope of Work

3.1 Project Structure Overview

The project is divided into three stages. This phased approach ensures that every decision in later stages is grounded in the strategic work completed during the foundation stage. The stages are:

  • Stage 1: Foundation (Discovery + Brand Identity + Strategy) -- covered in full by this Agreement
  • Stage 2: Design (Visual Design + Content Architecture) -- to be scoped during Stage 1
  • Stage 3: Build and Launch (Development + Testing + Deployment) -- to be scoped during Stage 1

3.2 Stage 1: Foundation -- Detailed Scope

Stage 1 comprises three core workstreams: Discovery, Brand Identity, and Digital Strategy. The Service Provider will deliver the following:

Workstream Deliverable Description
Discovery Discovery Call(s) In-depth conversations to understand the Client's businesses, goals, target audiences, competitors, and vision across all seven verticals.
Business Analysis Summary A written summary of findings from the discovery process, capturing the current state of each vertical and key opportunities.
Audience Profiles Detailed profiles for the target audiences of each business vertical, including demographics, behaviours, and needs.
Competitive Landscape Review An overview of key competitors for each vertical, noting what works well in the market and where the Client can differentiate.
Brand Identity Brand Strategy Document Core brand positioning, messaging framework, tone of voice guidelines, and brand values for the conglomerate and its verticals.
Visual Identity Direction Colour palettes, typography recommendations, and visual style exploration for the parent brand and sub-brands.
Logo Concepts Initial logo concepts for the parent brand (and guidance on how sub-brands relate visually). Up to [NUMBER] initial concepts, refined through the revision rounds described in Section 9.
Brand Guidelines Document A comprehensive guide showing how to apply the brand consistently across digital and print materials.
Digital Strategy Digital Presence Strategy A strategic plan for the Client's online presence, covering website architecture, content strategy, and how the seven verticals are best represented digitally.
Sitemap and Information Architecture Detailed sitemap(s) showing the proposed structure of the website(s), page hierarchy, and navigation approach.
Scope Definition for Stages 2 and 3 Based on the foundation work, a detailed proposal covering scope, deliverables, timelines, and pricing for the Design and Build stages.

3.3 Revenue Role Assessment

Stage 1 includes an assessment of the website's role in the Client's revenue generation strategy. If the assessment determines that a website is not the highest-value investment at this time, the Provider will advise accordingly and recommend alternative approaches. All other Stage 1 deliverables remain valid and are transferred to the Client upon receipt of full payment, regardless of the outcome of the Revenue Role assessment.

3.4 Stages 2 and 3: Future Scope

Stages 2 and 3 will be defined based on the strategic direction established during Stage 1. At the conclusion of Stage 1, the Service Provider will present a detailed proposal for each subsequent stage, including specific deliverables, timelines, and pricing.

Each future stage will be governed by a separate supplementary agreement that references this Agreement and adopts the same general terms (Sections 5 through 14) unless explicitly amended. Neither Party is obligated to proceed to subsequent stages. Proceeding to Stage 2 or Stage 3 requires mutual written agreement and a signed supplementary agreement for that stage.

3.5 What is Not Included

Unless explicitly added through a supplementary agreement, the following are outside the scope of this Agreement:

  • Copywriting or content creation (the Client is responsible for supplying written content, though the Service Provider may offer guidance on structure and tone)
  • Photography, videography, or stock image licensing
  • Third-party software subscriptions, domain registration, or hosting fees
  • Search engine optimisation (SEO) services beyond basic on-page recommendations
  • Ongoing website maintenance or support after launch
  • Social media management or advertising campaigns
  • Print design beyond what is specified in the brand guidelines deliverable
Section 4

Timeline

4.1 Stage 1 Estimated Duration

Stage 1 is estimated to take 3 to 4 weeks from the date work begins. The projected start date is [START DATE] and the projected completion date is [END DATE].

4.2 Factors Affecting the Timeline

The estimated duration assumes that the Client provides timely feedback, materials, and approvals as described in Section 8 (Client Responsibilities). Specifically, the timeline is based on the following assumptions:

Section 5

Investment and Payment Terms

5.1 Stage 1 Fee

Stage 1 (Foundation) Investment: ₦500,000 NGN (Nigerian Naira)

This is a fixed price for the complete Stage 1 scope of work as described in Section 3.2. The fee covers all deliverables listed, all communication and collaboration time, and up to two rounds of revisions per deliverable as outlined in Section 9. This fee is payable in full before work begins.

5.2 Payment Schedule

Payment for Stage 1 is due 100% upfront before work begins. The Service Provider will issue an invoice upon signing of this Agreement. Work will commence within 3 business days of the Service Provider confirming receipt of payment.

For Stages 2 and 3 (when scoped), the following payment schedule applies unless otherwise agreed in the relevant supplementary agreement:

  • Stage commencement: 50% of the stage fee, due before work begins on that stage.
  • Stage midpoint: 25% of the stage fee, due upon reaching the midpoint milestone as defined in the supplementary agreement.
  • Launch: 25% of the stage fee, due upon completion and launch of the deliverables for that stage.

5.3 Accepted Payment Methods

The Client may pay using the following methods:

  • Nigerian Bank Transfer: Payment to the Service Provider's Nigerian bank account. Account details will be provided on the invoice. This is the primary and preferred payment method.
  • Wise or International Transfer: Available as an alternative for international payments where Nigerian bank transfer is not feasible. Details will be provided on the invoice upon request.

5.4 Currency

All amounts in this Agreement are stated in Nigerian Naira (NGN, ₦). Invoices will be issued in NGN. Any currency conversion costs or bank transfer fees incurred by the Client are the Client's responsibility.

5.5 Late Payment

If payment is not received by the due date stated on the invoice, the following applies:

  • Project work will pause until outstanding payment is received. The Service Provider will notify the Client in writing that work has been paused due to non-payment.
  • The Service Provider is under no obligation to resume work or meet previously agreed timelines until full payment of the outstanding amount is confirmed.
  • If payment remains outstanding for more than 30 days past the due date, the Service Provider reserves the right to terminate this Agreement in accordance with Section 13.

5.6 Stages 2 and 3 Pricing

Fees for Stages 2 and 3 will be quoted separately in NGN as part of the scope definition deliverable at the end of Stage 1. Unless otherwise agreed, the default payment structure for Stages 2 and 3 is the milestone-based schedule described in Section 5.2 (50% at commencement, 25% at midpoint, 25% at launch). Payment terms for those stages will be confirmed in their respective supplementary agreements.

5.7 Additional Work

Any work requested by the Client that falls outside the scope defined in Section 3 will be quoted separately in writing before the work begins. The Service Provider will not undertake out-of-scope work without the Client's prior written approval of the additional fee.

Section 9

Revision Policy

9.1 Included Revisions

The Stage 1 fee includes up to 2 rounds of revisions per deliverable. A "round of revisions" means one consolidated set of feedback from the Client, followed by the Service Provider implementing the requested changes.

9.2 What Counts as a Revision

A revision is a change to a deliverable that has already been presented to the Client. Revisions include adjustments to design direction, content structure, colour choices, layout changes, and similar modifications to existing work. Revisions do not include entirely new concepts or a fundamentally different direction from what was discussed and agreed upon during the project. A request for a completely new approach would be considered additional scope and quoted separately.

9.3 Additional Revisions

If the Client requires more than 2 rounds of revisions on any deliverable, the Service Provider will provide a written quote for the additional revision work before proceeding. Additional revisions will be billed at [HOURLY RATE OR FIXED FEE PER ADDITIONAL ROUND] NGN. The Client must approve this quote in writing before the additional work begins.

9.4 Revision Turnaround

The Service Provider will complete each round of revisions within 3 to 5 business days of receiving consolidated feedback, depending on the complexity and volume of changes requested.

Section 11

Confidentiality

11.1 Mutual Obligations

Both Parties agree to keep confidential any information received from the other Party that is not publicly available. This includes, but is not limited to, business plans, financial information, client lists, technical data, strategies, and trade secrets. Both Parties will take reasonable steps to protect such information and will not disclose it to any third party without the other Party's prior written consent.

11.2 Client Business Information

The Service Provider acknowledges that they will have access to sensitive information about the Client's businesses, including details about the seven business verticals, financial performance, competitive strategies, customer data, and internal operations. The Service Provider agrees to treat all such information with the highest level of care and discretion. The Service Provider will not use the Client's business information for any purpose other than completing the work described in this Agreement.

11.3 Exceptions

The confidentiality obligations in this section do not apply to information that:

  • Is or becomes publicly available through no fault of the receiving Party.
  • Was already known to the receiving Party before being disclosed by the other Party.
  • Is independently developed by the receiving Party without use of the disclosing Party's confidential information.
  • Is required to be disclosed by law, regulation, or court order, provided that the disclosing Party is given reasonable prior notice where possible.

11.4 Duration

The confidentiality obligations in this section will survive the termination or expiry of this Agreement and will remain in effect for a period of 2 years after the last date on which either Party provided confidential information to the other.

11.5 Subcontractors

If the Service Provider engages any subcontractors to assist with the project (with the Client's prior knowledge), the Service Provider will ensure that those subcontractors are bound by confidentiality obligations that are at least as protective as those in this Agreement.

Section 12

Project Delays and Pauses

12.1 Client-Side Delays

If the Client causes a delay by not providing feedback, materials, approvals, or access within the timescales described in this Agreement, the project timeline will be adjusted to account for the delay. The Service Provider will notify the Client in writing when a delay is affecting the project schedule.

12.2 Extended Delays (Beyond 10 Business Days)

If any single delay on the Client's side extends beyond 10 business days, the Service Provider reserves the right to adjust the project timeline by a corresponding period, plus a reasonable amount of time to reorganise their schedule. The Service Provider will confirm the revised timeline in writing.

12.3 Project on Hold (30 Days)

If the Client does not respond to communications or provide necessary materials for a continuous period of 30 calendar days, the project will be placed on hold. The Service Provider will notify the Client in writing that the project has been placed on hold. While on hold:

  • No further work will be carried out.
  • The Service Provider is free to take on other projects and commitments.
  • The original timeline no longer applies.

To reactivate a project that has been on hold, the Client must contact the Service Provider in writing to request resumption. The Service Provider will then provide a revised timeline based on their current availability.

12.4 Reactivation After 60 Days

If the project has been on hold for 60 calendar days or more, the Service Provider may require a re-scoping fee to account for the time needed to review previous work, re-familiarise themselves with the project, and reorganise their schedule. This re-scoping fee will be quoted in writing and must be approved by the Client before work resumes. The re-scoping fee will not exceed 20% of the original Stage 1 fee unless the scope of work has materially changed during the pause.

12.5 Service Provider Delays

If the Service Provider is unable to meet a deadline due to illness, personal emergency, or other unforeseen circumstances, they will notify the Client as soon as reasonably possible and provide a revised delivery date. The Service Provider will make every reasonable effort to minimise disruption to the project.

Section 13

Termination

13.1 Termination by Either Party

Either Party may terminate this Agreement by providing the other Party with 14 days' written notice. Written notice must be sent by email to the address listed in Section 1 of this Agreement (or to an updated address provided in writing during the project).

13.2 Payment Upon Termination

If the Client terminates this Agreement, the Client will pay for all work completed up to the date of termination. If payment was made in advance (as is the case for Stage 1), the Service Provider will calculate the value of work completed based on the proportion of deliverables finished or substantially progressed, and will refund the difference between the amount paid and the value of work completed. The Service Provider will provide a written breakdown of this calculation within 10 business days of termination.

13.3 Termination by the Service Provider

If the Service Provider terminates this Agreement, the same payment calculation applies. The Service Provider will refund the Client for any work not yet completed. The Service Provider will make reasonable efforts to ensure an orderly handover, including providing all working files and documentation for the work completed to date.

13.4 Deliverables Upon Termination

Upon termination and receipt of appropriate payment, the Service Provider will transfer to the Client all completed deliverables and working files for the work that has been paid for. Work in progress that has not been paid for remains the property of the Service Provider.

13.5 Termination for Cause

Either Party may terminate this Agreement immediately (without the 14-day notice period) if the other Party commits a material breach of this Agreement and fails to remedy that breach within 10 business days of receiving written notice of the breach. A material breach includes, but is not limited to, non-payment, misuse of confidential information, or failure to perform fundamental obligations under this Agreement.

13.6 Surviving Clauses

The following sections will survive the termination or expiry of this Agreement: Section 10 (Intellectual Property), Section 11 (Confidentiality), Section 14 (Limitation of Liability), and Section 15 (Dispute Resolution).

Section 15

Dispute Resolution

15.1 Good Faith Negotiation

If any dispute or disagreement arises between the Parties in connection with this Agreement, the Parties agree to first attempt to resolve the matter through good faith negotiation. Either Party may initiate this process by sending a written notice to the other Party describing the dispute and requesting a discussion. Both Parties will make themselves available for a call or meeting within 10 business days of such notice and will make reasonable efforts to reach an amicable resolution.

15.2 Mediation

If the dispute cannot be resolved through good faith negotiation within 30 calendar days of the initial written notice, either Party may refer the matter to mediation. The Parties will agree on a mutually acceptable mediator. If they cannot agree on a mediator within 14 calendar days, either Party may request the appointment of a mediator through a recognised mediation body. The costs of mediation will be shared equally between the Parties, with each Party bearing their own legal costs.

15.3 Continued Performance

Unless the dispute relates to a matter that makes continued performance impossible, both Parties agree to continue performing their obligations under this Agreement while the dispute resolution process is underway.
