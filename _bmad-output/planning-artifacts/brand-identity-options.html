Design rationale: The stacked wordmark creates a visual hierarchy where "CITADEL" commands attention while "GLOBAL RESOURCES" provides context and "LIMITED" establishes corporate formality. The three-tier approach turns a four-word name into a focused brand statement. The generous letter-spacing in "CITADEL" conveys openness and confidence, the lighter-weight descriptor line sits above as a contextual anchor, and the quiet qualifier below completes the corporate identity. No symbol competes for attention; the typography does all the work.

Next Steps

Moving Forward

Select a preferred direction, or combine elements across directions. Each can be refined, iterated, and extended into a complete brand system including sub-brand lockups, stationery suite, digital assets, and environmental signage.

Direction 1: Best if the name alone should carry all brand equity. The three-tier stacked wordmark turns a long corporate name into a commanding typographic statement. Simplest to execute across media.

Direction 2: Best for digital-first flexibility. The GRC monogram serves as a strong standalone identifier on apps, social, and compact spaces.

Direction 3: Best for communicating growth and diversification visually. The abstract mark adds narrative depth without limiting scope.
